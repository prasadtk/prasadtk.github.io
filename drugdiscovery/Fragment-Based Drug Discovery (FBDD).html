Fragment-Based Drug Discovery (FBDD)

A modern paradigm for developing potent, high-quality therapeutics.

1. What is FBDD?

FBDD is a modern approach that starts by screening a library of very small, low-molecular-weight fragments. These fragments, typically defined by the **"Rule of 3,"** bind to a target protein with low affinity. The method's power lies in rationally building these weakly binding fragments into a highly potent and selective drug candidate.

2. High Hit Rate

Due to their small size, fragments have a higher chance of fitting into a protein's binding pocket. This leads to a much higher "hit rate" compared to traditional High-Throughput Screening (HTS), which tests larger, more complex molecules.

3. Molecular Efficiency

FBDD is based on the principle of **ligand efficiency**. Starting with a small, efficient fragment allows medicinal chemists to build an optimized, high-quality molecule with better properties from the ground up, reducing the risk of a poor drug.

4. Fragment Library Design

A small, diverse library of 500-2,000 fragments is carefully curated. These fragments are chosen to satisfy the **"Rule of 3"** and represent a wide range of chemical motifs, providing a solid foundation for drug design.

5. Screening for Hits

Because fragment binding is weak, highly sensitive **biophysical screening methods** are used to detect hits. Common techniques include NMR Spectroscopy, X-ray Crystallography, and Surface Plasmon Resonance (SPR).

6. Fragment Elaboration & Linking

Once validated, hit fragments are converted into a more potent lead compound. This is done either by **elaboration** (building upon the fragment) or **linking** two or more fragments together to create a single, more potent molecule.
